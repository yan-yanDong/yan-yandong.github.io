Research Statement

My research interests lie in the following four aspects:

  • Multi-armed bandits

  • Finite-length coding theory

  • Network coding and its applications

  • Theory in machine learning

I am broadly interested in information theory and online learning. During my doctoral studies, I focused on the open problem of verifying optimal finite-blocklength block codes under maximum likelihood (ML) decoding. In particular, I investigated optimal codes with four codewords over a wide range of blocklengths for binary symmetric channels. I also analyzed the scalability of communication performance in line networks as the number of hops grows, under both latency and buffer constraints. This analysis was conducted in two settings: line networks composed of general discrete memoryless channels (DMCs) and those affected by slow fading with outage events. In addition, I studied network utility optimization in multi-flow wireless networks employing network coding. As a postdoctoral researcher, I have expanded my research to include multi-armed bandit (MAB) problems, with a focus on both theoretical foundations and practical extensions involving constraints such as switching costs and delayed feedback.

Multi-armed bandits

  • Combinatorial Multi-Armed Bandit with Switching Cost

    I studied the combinatorial bandit problem with a cost associated with switching between selected arms in each round. I developed theoretical performance lower bounds and designed efficient algorithms with low regret for both bandit and semi-bandit feedback scenarios, while accounting for switching costs incurred when changing decisions between rounds.

  • Multi-Armed Bandit with Delays on the Reward Feedback

    Another focus of my research has been on multi-armed bandit problems where the reward feedback is delayed, aiming to design algorithms that efficiently handle such delays and minimize sample complexity of fixed-confidence best arm identification problem with the feedback latency.

Network Coding

  • Capacity of Line Networks with DMCs or slow-fading channels

    I conducted scalability analysis of throughput and latency as the line network length increases, comparing different coding schemes in a network affected by outages due to fading and interference. We compared the scalability of three schemes—random linear network coding, retransmission, and store-and-forward—providing valuable insights for the design of multihop wireless communication networks.

  • Network Utility Maximization with Network Coding

    I studied network utility optimization in multi-flow wireless networks when using BATS (BATched Sparse) codes—a variation of random linear network coding. We jointly optimized the BATS code parameters and network scheduling for all flows.

Optimal Finite-Blocklength Codes

  • Optimal Finite Length Codes for Binary Symmetric Channels (BSC)

    I have focused on codes with short block lengths, particularly designing block codes that maximize the ML decoding probability for BSC.
