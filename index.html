About me

I’m now a postoctorial fellow in the School of Science and Engineering (SSE) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK, Shenzhen).
Prior to this, I was a Research Fellow in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at the National University of Singapore (NUS), where I was hosted by Prof. Vincent Y. F. Tan.
In 2022, I obtained my Ph.D. from School of Science and Engineering (SSE) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK,Shenzhen) with Prof. Shenghao Yang as my advisor.
In July 2017, I obtained my Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Jilin University.

Research

Research interests

My research interests lie in the following three aspects:

  • Theory in Machine learning

  • Finite-length coding theory

  • Network communication

  • Online learning

In network communication, I explored ways to understand and improve network performance in constrained or unreliable environments, such as those with resource limitations or link reliability issues.
In finite-length coding, I investigated codes with short blocklengths, focusing on designing optimal block codes that maximize decoding probability for binary symmetric channels (BSC).
In online learning, I studied the theoretical analysis of the multi-armed bandit (MAB) problem with additional complexities, such as extra costs beyond the traditional setting or delayed feedback. Specifically, I developed theoretical performance lower bounds and designed efficient algorithms with low regret for both bandit and semi-bandit feedback scenarios, while accounting for switching costs incurred when changing decisions between rounds. Additionally, I am working on addressing the MAB problem in the presence of feedback delays. I am interested in the theoretical analysis in machine learning in terms of understanding machine learning algorithms from a information-theory perspective
