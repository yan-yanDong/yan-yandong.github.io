Research

Research interests

My research interests lie in the following three aspects:

  • Finite-length coding theory

  • Multi-armed bandits

  • Network coding and its applications

  • Theory in machine learning

In general, I am interested in the theoretical analysis in machine learning in terms of improving machine learning algorithms from a information-theory perspective including online learning and neural networks and finding the optimal short-length codes in terms of macimum likelihood (ML) decoding.

  • In finite-length coding, I investigated codes with short blocklengths, focusing on designing optimal block codes that maximize the ML decoding probability for binary symmetric channels (BSC).

  • In multi-armed bandits (MAB), I studied the theoretical analysis of the MAB problem with additional complexities, such as extra costs beyond the traditional setting or delayed feedback. Specifically, I developed theoretical performance lower bounds and designed efficient algorithms with low regret for both bandit and semi-bandit feedback scenarios, while accounting for switching costs incurred when changing decisions between rounds. Additionally, I am working on addressing the best arm identification (BAI) problem in the presence of feedback delays.

  • In network coding, I studied the network utility optimization problem when the BATS (BATched Sparse) codes (a class of random linear network coding variaiton) are employed in the multi-flow wireless networks. We jointly optimizes the BATS code parameters of all the flows and network scheduling. On the other hand, I performed the scalability analysis of the throughput and latency as the line network length increases when different coding schemes are employed in a network that suffers from outage events caused by fading and interference. We compare the scalability for three schemes: random linear network coding, retransmission and store-and-forward, which offers valuable insights for the design of multihop wireless communication networks.
