About me

I’m now a Postoctorial Fellow in the School of Science and Engineering (SSE) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK, Shenzhen).
Prior to this, I was a Research Fellow in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at the National University of Singapore (NUS), where I was hosted by Prof. Vincent Y. F. Tan.
In 2022, I earned my Ph.D. from School of Science and Engineering (SSE) at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHK,Shenzhen) supervised by Prof. Shenghao Yang.
In July 2017, I graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Jilin University.

Research

Research interests

My research interests lie in the following four aspects:

  • Finite-length coding theory

  • Multi-armed bandits

  • Network coding and its applications

  • Theory in machine learning

In general, I aim to improve machine learning algorithms from an information-theoretic perspective, focusing on areas like online learning and neural networks and address computational challenges in large language models (LLMs) using coding strategies. Additionally, I am interested in designing block codes with short block lengths that maintain high performance in maximum likelihood (ML) decoding.

  • In finite-length coding, I have focused on codes with short block lengths, particularly designing optimal block codes that maximize the ML decoding probability for binary symmetric channels (BSC).

  • In multi-armed bandits (MAB), I studied the theoretical analysis of the MAB problem with additional complexities, such as extra costs beyond the traditional setting or delayed feedback. Specifically, I developed theoretical performance lower bounds and designed efficient algorithms with low regret for both bandit and semi-bandit feedback scenarios, while accounting for switching costs incurred when changing decisions between rounds. Additionally, I am working on addressing the best arm identification (BAI) problem in the presence of feedback delays.

  • In network coding, I have worked on two major problems. First, I studied network utility optimization in multi-flow wireless networks when using BATS (BATched Sparse) codes—a variation of random linear network coding. We jointly optimized the BATS code parameters and network scheduling for all flows. Second, I conducted scalability analysis of throughput and latency as the line network length increases, comparing different coding schemes in a network affected by outages due to fading and interference. We compared the scalability of three schemes—random linear network coding, retransmission, and store-and-forward—providing valuable insights for the design of multihop wireless communication networks.
