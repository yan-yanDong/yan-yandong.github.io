Research

Research interests

My research interests lie in finite-length coding theory, network communication, and online learning. Previously, I explored ways to understand and improve network performance in constrained or unreliable environments, such as those with resource limitations or link reliability issues. In finite-length coding, I investigated codes with short blocklengths, focusing on designing optimal block codes that maximize decoding probability for binary symmetric channels (BSC). In online learning, I developed theoretical performance lower bounds and designed efficient algorithms with low regret for two feedback scenarios: bandit feedback and semi-bandit feedback.
